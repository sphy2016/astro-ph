[The Second Case of a Major Merger Triggering a Starburst in a Green Pea Galaxy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02527)
**Context/Aim** The authors used VLA to map HI 21 cm emission from a GP galaxy at z=0.0451. Based on their analysis, they find this system is a merger systems.
**Method/Data** The physical properties of this system is analyzed using the 21 cm image from VLA, the DECalS optical image, and SDSS spectra.
**Results:**
1. Based on the data from VLA, DECalS, and SDSS, they find the system is a merger systems. The GP has two neighbor and the 21 cm emission extends in an inverted "C" shape around the Green Pea and its companions, with the highest HI column density between the two neighboring galaxies. Although the velocity field and velocity dispersion images do not show clear merger signatures at the Green Pea location.
2. The HI mass of the GP and its immediate surrounding is ~10^9 Mass_sol, which is lower (x7.4) than the total HI mass of the system of three interacting galaxies. The HI depletion time of GP is ~0.69 Gyr, much shorter than that of typical galaxies at z=0.
3. They detected the damped LyA absorption and LyA emission from the GP, obtaining a high HI column density and a low LyA escape fraction

[Analysis of Multi-epoch JWST Images of ∼300 Little Red Dots: Tentative Detection of Variability in a Minority of Sources](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02729)
#Veryinteresting 
**Context/Aims** They conduct a comprehensive investigation of photometric variability of LRDs to verify the nature of such objects.
**Methods** 
- **Data**: the authors make the use of multi-epoch JWST observations in five deep fields: UDS, GOODS-S, GOODS-N, Abell 2744, and COSMOS, including all available JWST NIRCam data and multi-epoch JWST MIRI images.
- **Methods** With correction of systematic photometric zero point offsets and calibration of photometric uncertainties, we evaluate the variability of these LRDs using the distribution of the signal-to-noise ratio of their variability.
**Results**
- The SNR_var distributions of LRDs, including those with broad H-alpha/H-beta emission lines follow the standard Gaussian distribution. This result persists even including 28 LRDs with variability detected.
- Despite the overall lack of variability, they also find eight LRDs that show variability of SNRvar > 3, with variability amplitudes ranging from 0.24 to 0.82 mag. Two of them are reliable detections because of their relatively variability amplitude and coordinated trend of variability in different bands.
**Conclusions** The LRDs show very low variability amplitude intrinsically, which could be caused by super-Eddington accretion of the black holes in AGNs. The alternative scenario is they dominated by starburst.
