[Synergistic Radiative Transfer Modeling of MgII and Lyα Emission in Multiphase, Clumpy Galactic Environments: Application to Low-Redshift Lyman Continuum Leakers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11152)
#LyC #Important
**Aims** They used a Mg II and LyA RT model to reproduce the observed line profile of LyC leakers at z~0.3.
**Results:**
- For all 33 LyC leakers, they successfully reproduced MgII line profiles and found the necessary condition for strong LyC leakage: high maximum clump outflow velocity (>380 km s-1) and low total Mg II column density.
- For 6 LyC leakers with data available, they modeled the LyA profile but found that their spectral properties do not fully align with the conventional LyC leakage criteria. No clear correlation was identified between the modeled parameters and observed fesc. 
- About HI properties inferred from the RT model. The LyC leakage is primarily governed by the number of optically thick HI clumps per sightline. However, two leakers with lowest observed fesc exhibit the highest RT-inferred LyC escape fraction due to their lowest fcl value (showing double peak LyA escape fraction).
**Conclusions**: Our results support a "picket fence" geometry over a "density-bounded" scenario for the CGM, where a combination of high Mg II outflow velocities and low Mg II column densities may be correlated with the presence of more low-density HI channels that facilitate LyC escape.

[The evolution of [OIII]+Hβ equivalent width from z≃3−8: implications for the production and escape of ionizing photons during reionization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10988)

#EoR #Important 

**Aims** The authors select a group of galaxies at z=6.9-7.6 and z=3.2-3.6 and measure the equivalent width of OIII + H_beta. Using  $W_{\lambda}([O III]+H_{\beta})$  as a proxy of $\xi_{ion}$ and $\beta$ as a proxy of fesc, they find the ionizing photon budget at z>7 is dominated by galaxies with more moderate output, with a log($\xi_{ion}$/erg-1 Hz) =25.3 and fesc=0.05.
**Methods** 
- **Sample selection** 
	- **JWST sample** The JWST sample is selected based on Photometric redshift. The authors have made much efforts to get robust redshifts. The selection range of redshifts is from 6.9 to 7.2, where the F410M image probe $[O III]+H_{\beta}$ emission (N=289).
	- **VANDELS sample** The VANDELS sample is selected based on spectroscopic sample with the range of $3.2<z<3.6$ (N=253). They also require $3\sigma$ detection in K-band and $>3\sigma$  in at least one of the IRAC3 or IRAC4 band.
**Summary:**
- A clear apparent redshift evolution $W_{\lambda}([O iii]+H_{\beta})$, with the median increasing by a factor of 1.7 x from the VANDELS sample to the JWST sample.
- A clear broadening (∼ 0.12 dex) in the $W_{\lambda}([O iii]+H_{\beta})$, distribution is present for our high-redshift sample relative to that seen in VANDELS.
- MUV-faint galaxies, and those with redder UV slopes, have systematically weaker $[O\ III]+H_{\beta}$ emission. In contrast, we find that the lower-mass dwarf SFGs have the highest  $[O\ III]+H_{\beta}$.
- Our results constitute strengthened evidence for a systematic increase in the prevalence of EELGs ($W_{\lambda}([O iii]+H_{\beta})$≳ 1000 Å) with redshift. Overall, the EELG fraction increases from ∼ 4 per cent at $z \sim 2$, to ≃ 8 per cent in our VANDELS sample and $\sim$ 30 per cent in the PRIMER+JADES sample.
- The study finds a milder redshift evolution for the ionizing photon production efficiency (𝜉ion) compared to previous estimates, attributed to galaxies with less extreme 𝜉ion and greater scatter, which helps address concerns about high-redshift galaxies producing excess photons during reionization.
- Low-mass (log10 (𝑀∗/M⊙) ≲ 8) and relatively UV-bright (𝑀UV ≲ −19) galaxies with bluer UV continua (𝛽 ≲ −2.2) tend to have higher ionizing photon production efficiencies.

[Spectroscopic confirmation of a dust-obscured, metal-rich dwarf galaxy at z~5](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10954)
**Background** 
Only 1% of total mass of the interstellar medium is composed of dust. However, the presence of dust can also impact the derivation of physical properties and the understanding of galaxy evolution.
The dust-obscured objects dominate the cosmic star formation budget at least out to z=4-5 and the massive end of the stellar mass function. However, the properties of these object are not addressed well due to the faintness of these objects in the optical and the limitations of IR instruments before JWST.

See paragraph 3 for details of low mass dust obscured objects. 

**Aims** The paper present the spectroscopical confirmation of a low mass dust-obscured objects, which are photometrically selected from the CEERS (See formula 1 for selection criteria).
**Results**
- From the JWST spectrum, they detected multiple rest-frame optical lines (See Fig. 1). Based on these line, they derived the redshift of the object is 4.883 +- 0.003 and the dust extinction ranges from 2.2 to 3.3, depending on the dust reddening law. The value is extremely large at the stellar mass (log(M_sol/M) ~ 8.0-8.2, See Fig. 2  for details)
- Based on different metallicity tracers, they find the galaxy is also metal-rich, with 12 + log10(O/H) > 8.3. This is well above the expectation from both the mass-metallicity relation and the fundamental mass-metalliticy relation.
- The galaxy is undergoing a starburst (See Table 1) and the contribution of AGN is small (estimated from CIGALE). In addition, the size of the galaxy is comparable to the galaxies of similar stellar mass and redshift.

[Tracing star formation across cosmic time at tens of parsec-scales in the lensing cluster field Abell 2744](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10974)
**Aims** They have analysed 1956 individual clumps extracted from 478 galaxies at 0.7<z<10 using JWST/NIRCam data of the Abell 2744. The majority of the clumps (∼ 70%) studied in this work has gone so far undetected from the deep HST data available for this cluster.
**Methods** 
- **Galaxy selection** Galaxies have been selected according to their number of clumps and the availability of a robust redshift estimation, either from photometric or spectroscopic observations.
- **Clumps detection** The initial extraction has been performed with the software SExtractor and then a visual inspection on multiple JWST/NIRCam images of the selected galaxies has been performed to include fainter clumps missed and remove false detections.
- **Clumps measurements** The sizes are measured on a reference image depending on the redshift of the galaxies and corrected from the lensing magnification. The photometry has been extracted from 20 JWST/NIRcam filters. The physical properties have been derived from SED fitting.
**Results**:
- Thanks to the lensing magnifications, the star-forming regions are probed down to star clusters scales in size and mass. They classified clumps into three categories: unresolved (12%), compact (38%) and extended (48%). The late one impacts strongly the recovered size, brightness and stellar mass of the clumps, while the densities are only very weakly affected
- They find a significant evolution of the average clump ages, SFR, SFR surface densities and metallicity with increasing redshift, while median stellar mass and stellar mass surface densities are similar in the probed redshift range. The very high densities of high-redshift clumps suggests very dense clumps become more common at 3.5 ≤ 𝑧 < 5.5 and thus that star clusters are forming in environments that are more extreme as we move to higher redshift.
- A strong correlation is found between clump properties and those of their host galaxies, with more massive galaxies hosting more massive and older clumps. Clumps closer to the center of their host galaxies tend to be more massive, though there is no clear evidence of clump migration over time.
- Clumps at cosmic noon (around z∼2z \sim 2z∼2) populate the higher mass end of the mass function, corresponding to the peak of cosmic star formation history.
[Unveiling dust, molecular gas, and high star formation efficiency in extremely UV-bright star-forming galaxies at z∼2.1−3.6](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11121)
See the summary of this paper for a detailed information.
**Aims** The study aims to investigate the physical properties of 12 starburst galaxies at cosmic noon, focusing on their extreme UV brightness, star formation activity, gas and dust content.
**Methods** They analyzed the AMLA dust continuum and CO emission of these galaxies. The FIR dust continuum emission provides constraints on their IR luminosities, obscured SFR, and dust masses (9/12). The CO emission detected in 8 galaxies provides constraints on their molecular gas masses. Their also analyzed their H and Ks band images for UV morphology.
**Results**:
- **The FIR properties**
	- **Luminosity** The range of the FIR emission is 5.9-28.3x10^11 Lsol, with 6 galaxies remaining dominated by unobscured UV star formation rates (All LIRG-type galaxies). For half of galaxies, the obscured fractions of star formation are <25%, placing them a factor of two below the relation even below the UV-selected galaxies. The FIR dust continuum effective radii of the galaxies range from 1.7 kpc to 5 kpc.
	- **Morphology** The FIR morphology are more extended than UV with a mean R_eff,UV/R_eff,FIR = 0.43.
	- **Dust** The dust mass reaches M_dust = 2.3-11.1x10^7 Msol of the galaxies, support a efficient dust production scenario. SNe alone do barely produce enough dust during such a short timescale (~ 10 Myr) to reproduce the high M_dust and even fail for four galaxies unless a topheavy IMF is invoked.
- **The molecular gas**: The CO emission detected in 8 galaxies evidence molecular gas masses larger than stellar masses with a mean molecular gas.
	- Our extreme starburst galaxies are offset from the integrated KS M_molgas - SFR_UV+IR relation of main sequence galaxy and match the starburst regime as defined by Sagent et al. 2014. The high SFR_UV+IR with respect to their M_molgas yield unprecedentedly short molecular gas depletion timescales between 13 Myr and 71 Myr. On the other hand, their baryonic masses remain dominated by the cold molecular gas over stars, which results in very high molecular gas mass fractions between f_molgas , 60%-92%.
	- Dominated by very young stellar populations of ~10 Myr, we obtained direct star-formation efficiency measurements of our galaxies, ranging from 8% to 40%, much higher than <5% measured in nearby galaxies (The efficiency estimated here should be consider as lower limits, because not all CO detected cold gas involved SF).
- **Star formation**: The targeted galaxies are characterised by negligible dust attenuations with blue UV spectral slopes, , very young stellar populations of ~10 Myr, and and powerful starbursts with a high mean specific star formation rate of 112 Gyr-1 placing them ~ 1.5 dex above the main sequence at similar redshifts and stellar mass.
	- The resulting total SFRUV+IR bring these UV-bright galaxies considerably above (a factor of ∼ 30) the main sequence of star-forming galaxies at similar redshifts and stellar masses.
**Conclusions**: 
- These galaxies are likely caught at the very beginning of their stellar mass build-up and undergo a very efficient and fast conversion of gas into stars that can only result from the gas collapse within very short free-fall times. We find that the feedback-free starburst model seems to be able to explain the formation of these galaxies.
- To reconcile the co-spatial FIR dust emission with the UV-bright unattenuated emission, we speculate about radiation-driven outflows which can temporarily remove dust at the location of the starburst and expel dust at large distances in line with the measured large FIR effective radii.
[Direct High-resolution Observation of Feedback and Chemical Enrichment in the Circumgalactic Medium at Redshift 2.8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10993)
**Aims:** The authors report a detection of $\rm [O III]$ emission from a LyA nebular. The emission shows a spatial extension comparable to the LyA nebula, and a surprising filamentary morphology with high intrinsic surface brightness.
**Methods**:
- **Target description** The galaxy system SMM J02399-0136 at $z =2.808$ is lensed by Abell 370 with magnification $\mu = 2.38$. The lensing shear is orthogonal to the direction towards Abell 370. The sytem is known to be comprised of a broad absorption line QSO, a dusty star-forming galaxy, an irregular companion galaxy, and a projected companion. A large amount of "cool" CGM has been discovered surrounding this system, including a 100 kpc scale LyA nebula, as well as multiple evidences for extended ionized and molecular gas.
- **Emission line detection**: The emission line are detected based on the medium band F182M data. They subtract the continuum component from a interpolated image from adjacent F150W and F210M images.
**Results:** Here is a brief description about the results. See the paragraph 2 of Section 3 and Fig 2 for the $[O\ III]$  emission structure. The CGM gas resides in narrow filamentary structures with strong O iii emission, tracing the same extent as the LyA emission. The emitting gas is fully ionized and is energetically dominated by mechanical heating. The inferred density and pressure are higher than those commonly predicted by simulations of the CGM.
**Conclusions** The observed CGM emission originates from the gas expelled by the feedback processes, cooling down and enriching the CGM, while travelling to a distance of at least 60 kpc. It challenges numerical simulations to explain and reproduce the complex structures revealed by the observations.
