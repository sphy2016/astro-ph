# Research interests

This file guides how Codex selects and summarizes astro-ph papers for my personal reading diary.

The goal is not to cover all of astro-ph.
The goal is to find papers that are most relevant to my evolving research interests and summarize them in a compact, diary-like style.

## Must-not-miss topics

Always select papers if they are substantially about:

- massive quiescent galaxies
- high-redshift quiescent galaxies
- compact quiescent galaxies
- post-starburst galaxies
- early galaxy quenching
- formation histories of massive passive galaxies
- stellar ages, metallicities, abundance patterns, or star-formation histories of quiescent galaxies
- JWST/NIRSpec or NIRCam studies of massive quiescent galaxies
- progenitors of massive quiescent galaxies
- Lyman continuum / LyC surveys
- ionizing photon escape
- escape fraction / f_esc
- LyC leakers
- reionization-motivated galaxy samples
- observational LyC survey papers
- samples connecting LyC escape to dust, gas, feedback, morphology, or star formation

## Strong interests

Usually select papers related to:

- high-redshift galaxy evolution
- JWST studies of distant galaxies
- JWST/NIRSpec or NIRCam spectroscopy connected to galaxy evolution
- early massive galaxies
- stellar populations, ages, metallicities, and star-formation histories at high redshift
- chemical enrichment in early galaxies
- dust, gas, feedback, and outflows
- circumgalactic medium / intergalactic medium connections to galaxy evolution
- galaxy quenching and the buildup of passive populations
- simulations connected to galaxy formation and evolution
- reionization-era galaxy samples when connected to galaxy properties or LyC escape

## Secondary / selective interests

Select these only when the paper looks especially useful or connects clearly to stronger interests:

- general high-redshift galaxy candidates
- general JWST survey papers
- luminosity functions
- morphology studies
- photometric-redshift catalog papers
- broad cosmological simulations
- methods papers with clear galaxy-evolution applications

## Lower priority

Usually skip unless especially relevant:

- instrumentation-only papers
- solar physics
- planetary science
- gravitational-wave-only papers
- pure cosmology without a galaxy / CGM / IGM connection
- highly technical methods papers with no clear astro science application

## High-z and JWST selection rule

I am interested in high-z and JWST papers, but I cannot cover everything.

Do not select every high-z or JWST paper automatically.

Prioritize high-z / JWST papers when they connect to:

- galaxy evolution
- massive galaxies
- quiescent galaxies
- early quenching
- stellar populations
- LyC escape
- reionization
- CGM / IGM
- dust
- gas
- feedback
- outflows
- early structure formation

## Preferred note style

Keep notes compact and diary-like.

For selected papers, prefer:

- title with arXiv link
- one sentence explaining why the paper is relevant to my interests
- 2-5 concise bullets
- Aims / Methods / Results / Conclusions only when useful
- no hype
- no overclaiming beyond the abstract
- mark vague conclusions as tentative

The repo should remain a personal reading diary, not a formal digest site.
