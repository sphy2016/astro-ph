[The JWST EXCELS survey: Probing strong-line diagnostics and the chemical evolution of galaxies over cosmic time using Te-metallicities](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.10499)
**Aims:** The authors aims to access the strong-line calibration both in the local Universe and at high-redshift and the evolution of the mass-metallicity relation and fundamental-metallicity relation.
**Methods:** They presented Te-metallicity measurements of 22 galaxies at 1.65<z<7.92 with a median redshift. They also analyzed a high-quality same of 782 local galaxies selected from DESI ERS. Each of these galaxies were selected to have a detection of the [O III] auroral emission line. They also derived key physical properties for the galaxies in these samples, such as stellar mass, star formation rate, emission line fluxes, electron temperatures, oxygen abundances and nitrogen abundances.
**Results:** 
The key findings on strong line calibrations are:
1. Many strong-line calibrations heavily depend on the ionization parameters, therefore they cannot obtain reliable metallicities if the parameter are not taken into account.
2. The recently defined 𝑅diagnostic by Laseter et al. (2024) provides a reliable metallicity diagnostic with minimal scatter because it mostly removes the dependence on ionization state. Note that the R calibrations derived from this paper and Laseter et al. 2024 are different.
3. The authors presents a new calibrations, RNe, which is also ionization parameter independent. It can be used as an alternative when the R calibration is not available (z>9.5). Furthermore, the RNe calibration is insensitive to dust attenuation due to the close proximity of all the required emission lines.
4. The 𝑅 and 𝑅𝑁𝑒 are promising strong-line calibrations, however, there is still a region at 𝑅 ≳ 0.7 where these diagnostics are largely insensitive to metallicities.
5. Emission line calibrations based on the [Nii]𝜆6584 emission line may be biased at high-redshift due to systematic evolution of the N/O abundance ratio. We find that many of the EXCELS galaxies have moderately enhanced log(N/O)abundance ratios at fixed log(O/H) compared to local galaxies in our DESI EDR sample.This issue does not affect line diagnostics using neon or sulfur emission lines as these are like oxygen also alpha-elements.
6. Non-parametric metallicity calibrations such as those based on a KDE derived from a calibration sample provide competitive results to traditional parametric calibrations.
The key findings on the evolution of the MZR and FMR are:
1. We find that the EXCELS galaxies in our sample are located offset below the 𝑧=0 MZR at very similar stellar mass and metallicity to other [Oiii]𝜆4363 detected galaxies at 𝑧 > 2 in recent JWST and ground-based observations.
2. There may be a mild systematic evolution of the FMR as a function of redshift at z>4. However, similar to Nakajima et al. 2023 we find that the strength of this evolution is dependent on the FMR parametrisation used. The significance of this systematic offset from the FMR is low
3. On an individual basis we find several galaxies in our EXCELS and literature samples which are significantly offset from the local FMR at larger than 5 sigma significance. These galaxies , which we find particularly at z>3 may be a signature of an era in galaxy evolution where the equilibrium between gas accretion, chemical evolution and feedback processes has not yet been reached.
4. The reliable measurement of a redshift evolution of the FMR requires a homogeneous analysis of local and high-redshift galaxies with a large sample of 𝑇𝑒-metallicity measurements or unbiased strong-line calibrated metallicities.
**Conclusions:**  These growing observational capabilities at all redshifts will allow us to make significant improvements to strong-line metallicity calibrations which are applicable at all redshifts. These constraints on chemical evolution will also be an important probe of the baryon cycle in the early Universe.
[Spatially resolved dust properties over 50 kpc in a hyperluminous galaxy merger at z=4.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.10503)
**Aims:** The authors present the resolved dust-continuum ALMA observation from rest-frame 60 um-600 um of a hot DOG at z=4.6, which is interacting with at least three companion galaxies.
**Methods:** The authors derive the dust properties (e.g., mass, emissivity index, area of the emitting region, and temperature) in the hot DOG and resolved structures across a region of nearly ~ 50 kpc by modeling the multiwavelength ALMA observations of the dust continuum using a modified blackbody.
**Results:** 
1. **Heat from QSO** The dust temperature peaks at the location of ~110 K, which is likely heated by the central QSO, and drops to ~ 40K at a radius of 8 kpc, suggesting that heating by the QSO beyond that distance is nondominant.
2. **Heat from SF** The dust in the connecting streams between the host and companion galaxies is at temperatures between 30-40 K, typical of starburst galaxies, suggesting it is most likely heated by recent, in-situ star formation.
**Conclusions:** This is the first time dust properties are spatially resolved over several tens of kpc in a galaxy system beyond Cosmic Noon.

[Disturbed cold gas in galaxy and structure formation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14705)

**Aims:** The paper reports two absorber systems associating with nearby galaxies. By analysis the properties of these galaxies, the authors try to address the role played by cool and cold gas on the disk formation and the metal transformation.
**Methods:** One absorber is at the redshift of 4.9 and another is at the z=2.6. Observations were conducted with VLT/MUSE, JWST/NIRCam, and ALMA to detect Lya and nebular emission lines, as well as dust continuum emission in the vicinity of these two absorbing gas structures.
**Results:** 
1. **At z~4.9 absorber** They identify two LAE associated with a strong Mg II absorber pair separated by 1000 km s-1. The pair exhibits relative differences in metallicity, dust content, and ionization states, suggesting internal metal and dust exchange with the ultra-large cold gas structure.
2. **Absorber at z~2.6** They detect a dusty star-forming galaxy at a projected distance of D=38 kpc. This galaxy likely harbors a rotating disk, suggesting that cold gas at high-z plays a role in disk formation.
[A new prescription for the spectral properties of population III stellar populations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14028)
**Aims:** The study aims to investigate the emission properties of extremely low metallicity stellar populations in the EoR.
**Methods:** Employed the GALSEVN model to simulate spectral predictions for extremely low metallicity stars and explored emission-line diagnostics for differentiating population III (Pop III) stars from other primordial ionizing sources. Additional studies focused on the production of ionizing photons, Lyman-Werner photons, supernovae rates, and the gravitational-wave signals from binary black hole mergers concerning the Pop III.
**Results:** Found that certain emission-line diagnostics are effective in identifying Pop III stars, but their effectiveness diminishes for stellar-population ages over ∼1 Myr. The model also highlighted significant production rates of ionizing photons, influencing reionization, and Lyman-Werner photons, affecting star formation rates and chemical enrichment timescales through supernovae.
**Conclusions:** The study underscores the critical role of low-metallicity stellar populations in the EoR, particularly through their contribution to reionization and star formation processes.

[The Evolution of Size and Merger Fraction of Submillimeter Galaxies across 1<z≲6 as Observed by JWST](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15569)
**Aims:** The authors aim to study the formation history of SMGs by studying the evolution of sizes and merger fractions. The study investigates these evolutions over a broad redshift range of 1<z<6.
**Methods:** The sample used in this study comprises 222 SMGs identified by ALMA and JCMT. leveraging the high-resolution images delivered by NIRCam and MIRI, the sizes and merger/interaction fractions of SMGs are addressed.
- **The method of merger identification:** 
1. The major mergers are identified by two authors. They identify galaxies with distinct merger signatures, such as double nuclei, multicomponent structures, or companion galaxies within 15 kpc exhibiting tidal disturbances. If one galaxy is assigned as a merger by both of them, then it's a confirmed merger. If that only is assigned by one of them, then it's a tentative one. (R=15 kpc). The mirror mergers are identified using a similar method.
2. They also check the galaxies pair in their sample. They cross- match the sample with the CANDELS and COSMOS2020 catalog. If one companions falls in a range of 30 kpc of galaxies, then the galaxies is considered as a tentative galaxies pair. These pairs are further confirmed if the phot-z or spec-z information is available.
**Results:** 
- They find significant evolution in Re in rest-frame V band and NIR band with the NIR size evolution resembling that of massive SFGs at low-z.
- They find a major merger fraction of 24.3 ±3.7% and an interaction fraction of up to 48.4 ±11.1%. The major merger fraction exhibits an increase from 14.7±9.1% at z= 1 to 26.6±8.4% at z = 3, after which it remains approximately constant across the redshift range 3 < z < 6. In contrast, the interaction fraction remains relatively stable across the range 2 < z < 5.
**Conclusions:**
1. The late-stage major mergers are not the primary formation mechanism for SMGs at z <3, while interactions appear to play a significant role across the broader redshift range of 1 < z < 6.
2. HST-based major merger identifications may overestimate the true fraction by a factor of 1.7 at z ∼2.
