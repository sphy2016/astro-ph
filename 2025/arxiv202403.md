[The Physical Properties and Morphologies of Faint Dusty Star-forming Galaxies Identified with JWST](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.00102)
**Aims:** The authors aims to study the properties of the faint dusty star-forming galaxies in terms of physical properties and morphologies.
**Methods:** The authors develop a sample of 234 dusty star-forming galaxies from the A2744 and the GOODS-S fields as priors for SCUBA-2 measurements. The selected 47 bright and 187 faint DSFGs based on submillimeter flux.
**Results:**
1. They identify a tight negative correlation between redshift and both F444W and F150W, suggesting that the observed NIR flux could be an effective way of selecting high-z DSFGs.
2. The two populations of bright and faint DSFGs have similar properties expect that the faint DSFGs have the lower SFRs and total infrared luminosities. The stellar masses do not appear to be strongly dependent on either the SFRs or the submillimeter flux.
3. These results suggest that the faint DSFGs are drawn from the same population of galaxies as the bright DSFGs.
4. We find a lower merger fraction (∼ 21%) relative to previous HST-based studies.
**Conclusions:** These results suggest that the faint DSFGs are drawn from the same population of galaxies as the bright DSFGs.

[Tentative detection of neutral gas in a Little Red Dot at z=4.46](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.00998)
**Aims:** The authors aims to explore the molecular and neutral gas properties of LRDs at high-z. These AGNs are noted for their red colors and significant Balmer breaks, including old populations in their host galaxies.
**Methods:** The research utilized ALMA observations targeting molecular gas through CO(7-6) and neutral gas via CI emissions in three LRDs located at redshifts z=3.10, 4.46, and 7.04. The primary goal was to determine the molecular gas mass and understand the dynamical mass of these galaxies.
**Results:** The study did not detect CO in any of the targeted LRDs, setting upper limits on their molecular gas mass to be less than 1-5×10^10 M⊙. A tentative (4.9σ) detection of the CI line was reported in A2744-45924 at z=4.46, indicating a narrow line width (FWHM ∼80 km/s) and a dynamical mass significantly lower than expected, suggesting potential discrepancies in mass estimation methods or galaxy properties.
**Conclusions:** These findings suggest that LRDs may significantly differ from typical high-z quasars, which are generally associated with massive, gas-rich, and actively star-forming host galaxies. The lower dynamical mass and lack of significant molecular gas in LRDs might point to a non-stellar origin of the observed Balmer breaks, challenging existing models of AGN-host galaxy evolution at high redshifts.
